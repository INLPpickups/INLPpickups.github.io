# So, what is an INLP anyways?

Well, we're a pickup company. We have not created a physical pickup yet, but I'd say we've made some pretty good designs. Here's what I wrote for our 'Public Research Endeavour'. 

INLP Pickups' "Quad-Pods" are similar to the standard humbucker or stacked single coil, but with 4 coils (each encompassing ~3-4 strings) instead of 2 coils (each encompassing 6-8 strings). This allows for 3 distinct benefits right off the bat.

First, the narrower coil can fit in more winds than the standard 6-8 string coil with the same amount of wire. This raises the 'inductance' of the pickup, leading to a higher output & a much more intricate way to voice how "bassy" or "trebly" each pickup is (remember: a pickup's output can be raised by increasing magnetic strength and/or the number of windings, but increased magnetic strength adds treble & an increased number of windings reduces it!).

Second, due to the magnetic field & coil combinations shared across the pickup, it makes it possible to split from a standard humbucker to a hum-free 'single' coil. But as opposed to most noiseless 'single-coils' (which produce a sound that, while still very nice, could be seen as a 'compromise' for no hum- although as always, YMMV), we do a split similar to a P-Bass pickup, with one coil higher than the other. This reduces the 'muddiness' resulting from an overlap of string spread & location in ordinary stacked or dual-rail noiseless 'single' coils. (One thing to note however, is that this wouldn't work with a most sustainer pickups: ironically, we've had to create a few 'compromises': either run it in a non-noiseless fashion (because you don't really have to worry about a cranked-up noise gate killing your sustain OR harmonics anymore) or use what we've dubbed as Voice 3... more on that below.)

And third: Because we have 4 coils, we can now mix-and-match all sorts of series & parallel combinations of the coils, resulting in the creation of 6 unique voices for you to choose from (or switch between, if you'd like to! as before, select how many voices you want to switch from later on in the questionnaire). They are:

1 - Smaller magnetic field by itself, lower winding-to-magnetic-force ratio (e.g. strat pickup)
2 - Smaller magnetic field by itself, higher winding-to-magnetic-force ratio (e.g. hot rails, hot stack or hot single coil)
3 - Larger magnetic field in parallel, lower winding-to-magnetic-force ratio (e.g. Rickenbacker Toaster Pickup)
4 - Larger magnetic field in parallel, higher winding-to-magnetic-force ratio (e.g. P90, Jazzmaster Pickup)
5 - Larger magnetic field in series, lower winding-to-magnetic-force ratio (e.g. PAF, Super Distortion)
6 - Larger magnetic field in series, higher winding-to-magnetic-force ratio (e.g. D-Activator, 'Frankenbucker', El Diablo, Dirty Fingers)
